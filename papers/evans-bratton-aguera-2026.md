# Agentic AI and the Next Intelligence Explosion

- **Authors:** James Evans, Benjamin Bratton, Blaise Agüera y Arcas
- **Source:** Science, Vol 391, Issue 6791, March 19 2026
- **DOI:** 10.1126/science.aeg1895

## Key Claims

1. Intelligence is plural, social, and relational — not a single scalar quantity
2. Frontier reasoning models spontaneously develop "society of thought" — multi-agent-like internal debates
3. Robust reasoning is a social process, even within a single mind
4. Each prior intelligence explosion was emergence of a new socially aggregated unit of cognition
5. The path to more powerful AI runs through composing richer social systems, not bigger individual models
6. Human-AI "centaur" configurations are the operational unit
7. Agent institutions (role protocols, governance structures) matter more than individual agent capability
8. Institutional alignment > dyadic RLHF for scalable governance
9. Conflict and oversight must be architectural, not aspirational

## Testable Hypotheses for RATiMICS

- H1: Adversarial review (two agents with opposing mandates) catches more bugs than single-agent review
- H2: Spawning multiple agents with different strategies on complex issues produces better outcomes than single-agent
- H3: Structured post-mortem memory reduces repeat failures across agent runs
- H4: Role protocol abstraction enables agent substitutability without quality loss
- H5: Cross-repo knowledge sharing reduces agent context-gathering time on multi-repo tasks
- H6: Constitutional check agents (explicit policy mandates) catch compliance issues that general reviewers miss
